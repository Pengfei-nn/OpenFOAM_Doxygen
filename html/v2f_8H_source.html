33 Description
34  Lien and Kalitzin's v2-f turbulence model for incompressible and
35  compressible flows, with a limit imposed on the turbulent viscosity given
36  by Davidson et al.
37 
38  The model solves for turbulence kinetic energy k and turbulence dissipation
39  rate epsilon, with additional equations for the turbulence stress normal to
40  streamlines, v2, and elliptic damping function, f.
41 
42  The variant implemented employs N=6, such that f=0 on walls.
43 
44  Wall boundary conditions are:
45 
46  k = kLowReWallFunction
47  epsilon = epsilonWallFunction
48  v2 = v2WallFunction
49  f = fWallFunction
50 
51  These are applicable to both low- and high-Reynolds number flows.
52 
53  Inlet values can be approximated by:
54 
55  v2 = 2/3 k
56  f = zero-gradient
57 
58  References:
59  \verbatim
60  Lien, F. S., & Kalitzin, G. (2001).
61  Computations of transonic flow with the v2f turbulence model.
62  International Journal of Heat and Fluid Flow, 22(1), 53-61.
63 
64  Davidson, L., Nielsen, P., & Sveningsson, A. (2003).
65  Modifications of the v2-f model for computing the flow in a
66  3D wall jet.
67  Turbulence, Heat and Mass Transfer, 4, 577-584
68  \endverbatim
69 
70  The default model coefficients are
71  \verbatim
72  v2fCoeffs
73  {
74  Cmu 0.22;
75  CmuKEps 0.09;
76  C1 1.4;
77  C2 0.3;
78  CL 0.23;
79  Ceta 70;
80  Ceps2 1.9;
81  Ceps3 -0.33;
82  sigmaEps 1.3;
83  sigmaK 1;
84  }
85  \endverbatim
86 
87 Note
88  If the kLowReWallFunction is employed, a velocity variant of the turbulent
89  viscosity wall function should be used, e.g. nutUWallFunction. Turbulence
90  k variants (nutk...) for this case will not behave correctly.
91 
92 See also
93  Foam::RASModels::v2fBase
